# Laws of UX – Checklist

- Aesthetic‑Usability: clean visuals, no blockers on core flows
- Fitts’s Law: targets ≥ 44×44, adequate spacing
- Hick’s Law: minimize choices; clear defaults
- Jakob’s Law: follow web conventions; predictable navigation
- Miller’s Law: chunk content; readable at all breakpoints
- Parkinson’s Law: shortest path for primary goal
- Peak‑End Rule: strong first impression and endings (CTAs, success states)
- Serial Position Effect: key info at start/end
- Tesler’s Law: hide complexity, good defaults, progressive disclosure
- Von Restorff: primary CTAs stand out (color/size/space)
